The Meaning of Valentine’s Day

Now that I’m forty years old and married with kids, Valentine’s Day doesn’t really mean the same to me. Sure, I remembered that it was coming up on Saturday, and my wife and I did try to do something special. We knew we’d have our 2-year-old son Elijah with us, so we talked about eating lunch out. But, then Elijah slept right through lunch, and I was working from home, so we settled on getting food from McDonald’s.

I think that pretty much summarizes what Valentine’s Day means for me. First of all, I do totally believe in the spirit of this holiday. Second, I don’t subscribe to the idea that you should go out of your way to tell someone you love them on February 14th just because its expected from you. If you have people in your life who you appreciate, you should express yourself whenever you want. Don’t wait for a holiday. I hope nobody actually waits.

The ironic thing about Valentine’s Day is that kids really get into it, and they don’t really understand what love is all about. Maybe that’s for the best. When I was in elementary school, we would pass out Valentine’s Day cards. There was a little bit of drama over who did or didn’t get a card. Sometimes, it seemed important when I got a card from a girl who I really “liked.” My oldest two kids went through the same thing in elementary school. Last year was the last year that we bought Valentine’s Day cards for our son, Josh, to pass out in class. This year, he attended a Valentine’s Day dance at the middle school.

As a parent, the other great thing about Valentine’s Day are the Daddy-Daughter dances. It’s been a while since we attended our last one, but I have a lot of great memories from the dances that my daughter Kayla and I attended. I remember taking her when she was in the first grade and having to lift her up just so that we could dance. In only a few years, she could stand nose-to-nose with me without any effort at all.
